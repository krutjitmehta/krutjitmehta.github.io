Teaching

Courses taught and assisted at the university level in electrical engineering, signal processing, and mathematics.

I believe the most effective engineering education relies on conceptual understanding rather than memorization. In signal processin and systems courses especially, students benefit far more from understanding why a transform or algorithm works than from memorizing its steps. My goal in every classroom setting — whether as a TA or lecturer — is to build that bridge, and to make the material accessible to students across a wide range of mathematical backgrounds.

Approach & Methods

Intuition First

I lead with the physical or geometric meaning of a concept before introducing notation, so students know what they're formalizing.

Computation in the Loop

MATLAB and Python examples run alongside theory — students verify formulas numerically before trusting them analytically.

Active Office Hours

Office hours are problem-solving sessions, not just Q&A. I ask students to articulate where their understanding breaks down before working through it together.

Exam Design

I write problems that test reasoning, not recall — students should be able to reconstruct a result from first principles rather than retrieve a memorized formula.
